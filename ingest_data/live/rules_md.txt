# QRT.C3 Grand Final — Game Rules

Your player competes in a repeated, multi-player, matrix-payoff variant of
Rock-Paper-Scissors. Each team writes a program ("an agent") that talks to
the game server over HTTP. Read these rules carefully — they describe what
the server expects from your agent and how scoring works.

## Tournament structure

- A **game** is a sequence of rounds played against the same set of
  opponents. The tournament runs until the wall-clock limit; you don't
  know in advance exactly how many rounds you'll play.
- Each round you may choose to **opt in** (play this round) or **sit out**.
  Sit-outs miss the pair payoffs and the per-participant participation
  payment. They also cannot place a bet on the round's **betting market**
  (see below). Sit-outs still see the game state and can return next round.
- Each round draws a fresh 3×3 integer payoff matrix. Every cell is
  sampled **independently and uniformly** from `[-3, 3]`. Any matrix is
  possible: constant rows, dominated actions, degenerate games,
  all-positive or all-negative payoffs. Plan for the full space, not just
  "nice" matrices.
- The matrix is partially **masked** in later rounds; hidden cells appear
  as `null` in your `payoff_matrix`. The average number of masked entries
  grows as the round index increases, so later rounds typically reveal less
  upfront. **A masked cell becomes public the first time any pair plays
  it** ("reveal-on-play"); any remaining masks are revealed at round close.
- All matches in a round run **in parallel**. If `K` teams opt into the
  round, you are matched against every other joiner — `K − 1` simultaneous
  pair matches sharing the same matrix.

## Per-round phases

| Phase | Tournament duration | What you can submit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `ROUND_OPT_IN`     | 5 s    | `/participate` (with optional bet) |
| `ROUND_BROADCAST`  | 10 s   | `/broadcast` |
| `ROUND_THINKING`   | 15 s   | — (silent strategy window) |
| `ROUND_PLAYING`    | 2 s per turn × 6–12 turns | `/action` |
| `ROUND_COMPLETE`   | instant | — |
| `ROUND_PAUSE`      | 5 s    | (no submissions; update your strategy between rounds) |

The durations above are the values configured for the live tournament;
the operator may retune them between stages. **Do not hard-code them.**
Poll `/state` and read `*_deadline_unix_ms` against `server_now_unix_ms`
to learn the real remaining time for the current phase. Each phase accepts
only specific endpoints — submitting the wrong endpoint for the current
phase returns an error.

### Opt-in (`ROUND_OPT_IN`)

The masked matrix, the per-participant participation reward, and the
round's **bet proposition** are published. The proposition is a short
parseable string like `"R>=*"` ("rock will be the modal action this
round") — see [The betting market](#the-betting-market) for the full
grammar and settlement rules. You decide whether to play this round and
(optionally) whether to bet on the proposition by POSTing:

```json
POST /participate {
  "round_index": <int>,
  "join": true|false,
  "agree": true|false        // optional; only meaningful when join=true
}
```

- Every joiner is paid `PARTICIPATION_REWARD` (default `5`) at round close.
- `agree: true` bets that the round's proposition will hold; `agree: false`
  bets against. Each bettor stakes `BET_STAKE` (default `15`). Settlement
  is at round close (see "The betting market" below). Omitting `agree` is
  an abstention — you play normally without staking.
- If you do not submit before the deadline you are treated as
  `join: false` (and therefore cannot bet).
- Choice is final once submitted.
- Sit-outs receive no participation payment and **cannot bet**. They still
  see the round via `/state` (`phase: "idle"`) and `/logs`.

### Broadcast (`ROUND_BROADCAST`)

Joiners send a single public message:

```json
POST /broadcast {
  "round_index": <int>,
  "message": "I will reward cooperation."
}
```

- ≤ `280` chars. Longer payloads are rejected.
- Broadcasts are **public**: every joiner sees every other joiner's
  message during the round on the dashboard's live broadcast pane, and
  they're posted to `/logs` at `ROUND_COMPLETE`. If you want to address a
  specific opponent, name them in the text.
- Non-binding cheap talk — you don't have to follow through on what you
  say. But repeated lies leak via observable play and damage your
  credibility.
- Omitting `/broadcast` entirely is fine (empty message recorded).
- First valid payload wins; further POSTs are rejected.

### Thinking (`ROUND_THINKING`)

A silent strategy window. `/broadcast` and `/action` are closed. Use this
window to plan your action sequence for the upcoming turns, model opponents
from broadcasts, etc. The window is a fixed wall-clock duration regardless
of how fast others finished — you cannot shorten it.

### Action turns (`ROUND_PLAYING`)

Each turn you POST one payload with a per-opponent actions dict:

```json
POST /action {
  "round_index": <int>,
  "turn_index": <int>,
  "actions": { "<opp_id>": 0|1|2, ... }
}
```

- Different actions per opponent are allowed and often optimal — you can
  cooperate with one and exploit another in the same turn.
- A missing per-opponent entry → random action substituted + `-1`
  penalty, **for that pair only**. Your other matchups are unaffected.
- First valid payload wins; later POSTs for the same turn are rejected.
- Turns are **round-synchronous**: turn `T+1` does not start until turn
  `T` has resolved for every active pair.

#### Stochastic stopping

Each round runs between **6 and 12 turns** — the exact stopping turn is
not fixed. The signal that the round is about to end is
`next_turn_final: true` in `/state`: when you see that flag, the turn
you're about to submit is the last one. Don't try to count turns
yourself; rely on the flag.

A `next_turn_final: true` turn is a real strategic moment — cooperative
arrangements that relied on the shadow of the future tend to unravel
here.

### Round close (`ROUND_COMPLETE`)

- Per-pair payoffs and penalties are summed into your total score.
- Each joiner is paid `PARTICIPATION_REWARD` (flat, per-participant).
- The **betting market** settles (winners share losers' stakes).
- Any matrix cells that went unplayed during the round are revealed.
- Broadcasts are released into `/logs`.

## Payoff rule (symmetric perspective — important)

If you play action `i` and an opponent plays `j`, your payoff is `A[i][j]`
and their payoff is `A[j][i]`. Both players read the same matrix from
their own perspective.

The game is generally **non-zero-sum**: `A[i][j] + A[j][i]` need not be
0. You can both win, both lose, or asymmetrically gain.

**Common mistake to avoid:** do *not* assume the opponent's payoff is
`-A[i][j]`. Compute it as `A[j][i]`.

## The betting market

At round setup the server publishes a single **bet proposition** — a claim
about how this round's action submissions will tally. Joiners can agree
or disagree with the claim by including `"agree": true|false` in their
`/participate` payload. The proposition is exposed at `/state.bet_proposition`
and on the dashboard.

### Proposition grammar

The proposition is a short, parseable string. Three forms:

| Form | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `X>=*` | Action `X` has at least as many submissions as every other action this round (i.e. `X` is the modal action; ties OK). |
| `X>Y`  | Strictly more `X` submissions than `Y` submissions. |
| `X>=Y` | At-least-as-many `X` submissions as `Y` submissions. |

`X` and `Y` are letters in `{R, P, S}`, mapping to actions `{0, 1, 2}`
respectively. The full pool of 15 propositions:

```
R>=* P>=* S>=*           ← modal-action claims
R>P  R>S  P>R  P>S  S>R  S>P    ← strict pairwise
R>=P R>=S P>=R P>=S S>=R S>=P   ← weak pairwise
```

One proposition is drawn at round setup and exposed via `bet_proposition`.

### Stake and settlement

- Each bettor stakes `BET_STAKE` points (default `15`).
- At round close, the server counts every action submission across every
  pair and every turn in the round (including random substitutes for
  missed actions). It evaluates the proposition against the per-action
  totals and produces a TRUE/FALSE resolution.
- Winners (bettors whose stance matches the truth value) get **their stake
  back plus** an equal share of the losers' pooled stakes.
- Losers (bettors on the wrong side) forfeit their stake.
- **One-sided market — all winners:** every bettor agreed on the right side.
  No loser pool to share — stakes are refunded (net delta = 0).
- **One-sided market — all losers:** every bettor agreed on the wrong side.
  There are no winners to pay, so the stakes forfeit to the house (sink).
  Your net delta is `−BET_STAKE`.
- **Abstention** (joined the round but omitted `agree`): no stake, no
  payout. Neutral.

## Scoring summary

Your total score across the tournament is the sum of:

- **Pay** — per-pair payoffs from resolved turns (`A[my_action][opp_action]`).
- **Pot** — per-round `PARTICIPATION_REWARD` if you joined the round, else `0`.
- **Bet** — net betting-market payouts (sum of `bet_payout` over all rounds;
  +ve for a winning bet's share of the loser pool, −`BET_STAKE` for a
  losing bet, `0` for abstentions or refunded one-sided markets).
- **Pen** — penalties (`-1` per missed or invalid action, per affected pair).

The leaderboard breaks total score into these four columns and ranks
players by total.
